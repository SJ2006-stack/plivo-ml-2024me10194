1. Problem — a product constraint disguised as ML

At every annotated pause a voice agent must decide: user done (EOT) or mid-thought (hold)? Too early → interruption. Too late → awkward silence. Contest metric = mean response delay (ms) at ≤5% interrupted turns (lower better). Hidden test is mostly Hindi.

Causal hardline: features may use only audio from time 0 to pause_start. Never pause duration. Never post-pause audio. Never pause_end as a feature or as a sample weight. That constraint is why silence timers are the status quo — and why beating them is impressive.

Silence is already strong on Hindi (~850 ms) because true EOTs are short, and weak on English (~1600 ms timeout). Beating both means real hold/eot ranking, not a language-specific cheat.

2. Solution — what we ship

2.1 Signal — Tier-1 causal prosody (born from listening)

  • librosa.piptrack F0 (fmin=50, mag>0.5) — kept after pyin failed honestly
  • Energy decay / drop into the pause; pre-pause RMS ratios
  • Final lengthening & speaking-rate cues
  • Relative final pitch → fall / rise scores
  • Spectral shape + ZCR; turn structure (pause_index, IPI, first/late flags)
  • Listening gates: soften rise; cut fall boost on first pause (phrase-final ≠ turn-final); Hindi ×1.10 safe nudge

Hypotheses came from ears (en__051 fall→EOT, en__066 hang→HOLD, en__082 first-pause false cutoff, hi__002 rising short EOT). Code implemented; listening invented.

2.2 Model & inference

One unified EN+HI ensemble: logistic regression + shallow HistGradientBoosting, class-balanced weights without pause-duration leakage, trained on the full handout for the submission artifact → starter/models/unified.joblib.

4. Why we beat status quo

English — hundreds of ms sooner

Silence waits the full 1600 ms timeout. Our ship answers at 1000 ms with real hold/eot ranking from pitch fall, energy decay, and lengthening — inside the ≤5% interruption budget. Even the honest freeze (~1300 ms) still beats silence. That is not a formatting trick; that is earlier floor-taking after true EOTs.

Hindi — beat a strong timer

Silence is already strong at 850 ms. Handout ship reaches 781 ms (AUC ~0.86). Best trusted holdout remains pre-unified OOF ~840 ms. We claim both: the ship win and the generalization bar — not one pretending to be the other.

5. Timeline — how we got here

  1. Baselines — silence EN 1600 / HI 850; weak mine EN 1190 / HI 850 (AUC↑ delay flat on Hindi).
  2. Fake glory — Tier-1 + fat ensemble → 298 / 398 in-sample. Felt solved. Was not.
  3. Honesty collapse — 70/30 + OOF: EN ~1229–1300, HI ~800–840. Weight leak found; model shrunk.
  4. Listening — observation.md: fall, hang, phrase≠turn, rise-on-short-EOT.
  5. pyin failure — HI OOF 850 / AUC 0.653; revert piptrack.
  6. Unified train — one EN+HI model; protocol freeze thr=0.8 timeouts Hindi (1600).
  7. Full-data ship — EN 1015 / HI 783 (first clear HI silence beat on handout).
  8. Surgical gates — EN 1000; reject HI-772/EN-1030; Hindi ×1.10 → 1000 / 781.

6. Failures we faced (and converted into assets)

  1. In-sample overfit — 298/398 looked god-tier; OOF destroyed it. Built turn-grouped splits / protocol.
  2. Pause-duration sample weights — future leak in training (corr≈0.9 with hold duration); removed.
  3. Over-capacity RF/ET — memorized ~200 turns; shrunk to LR+shallow HGB.
  4. Metric ≠ AUC — Hindi AUC rose for hours while delay stuck at 850.
  5. pyin pitch “upgrade” — HI OOF 850 / AUC 0.653; reverted to piptrack.
  6. Val OP instability — 15% cutoffs or thr=0.8 timeout (1600 ms). Both are val lies.
  7. Phrase-final ≠ turn-final — first-pause false cutoffs (en__082); gated fall boost.
  8. Rise hurts short EOTs — hi__002 family; softened rise on first pause.
  9. HI-772 vanity — English died to 1030; rejected. Combined objective > single-lang glory.
  10. m2 single-LR — parallel churn; abandoned to protect the working path.
Integrity is part of the win. We explicitly refuse to call 298/398 “solved endpointing.” A deliverable that hides its cemetery of bad ideas is a weaker deliverable.

7. What we learned going deep

Science / method

  • Ears before architecture. Listening beats clever trackers that break slopes.
  • Causality is a product constraint, not a checkbox.
  • Group by turn_id or you leak speaker/rhythm across pauses.
  • Optimize the scorer, not ROC. Hindi will gaslight you with AUCs.
  • Complexity ≠ progress. pyin was “better” and worse.

Contest craft

  • Small late gates > late rewrites. First-pause gates + ×1.10 nudge beat another full retrain.
  • Protect the combined objective. Best-HI-alone ≠ best-ship if English regresses.
  • Document INVALID runs. Graders (and future you) need the cemetery.
  • Freeze OP carefully on tiny val — or timeout / over-interrupt.
  • Ship one CSV for both langs; reduce grader friction.

9. Deep learnings — the shortlist we would tattoo on a whiteboard

  1. If a number makes you feel like a genius before holdout, it is probably lying.
  2. Pause duration explains silence failures to humans and still cannot be a causal feature.
  3. Hindi AUC can rise while contest delay stays glued to 850 — believe the scorer.
  4. Phrase-final prosody is not turn-final intent — gate first pauses.
  5. Late in the contest, a ×1.10 language-safe nudge can beat another architecture rewrite.
  6. Rejecting HI-772 to keep EN-1000 was a win, not a loss.
  7. Write the cemetery. Glory without integrity is just another INVALID row.
